FACTS ABOUT NATURE

Nature, with its diverse and fascinating elements, holds numerous intriguing facts that showcase its beauty and complexity:
1. **Biodiversity:** Earth is home to an estimated 8.7 million species, ranging from microscopic bacteria to giant whales, each playing a unique role in the ecosystem.
2. **Photosynthesis:** Plants engage in photosynthesis, a process where they convert sunlight into energy, producing oxygen as a byproduct. This process sustains almost all life forms on Earth.
3. **Water World:** About 71% of the Earth's surface is covered in water, with oceans holding approximately 97% of the planetâ€™s water.
4. **Amazon Rainforest:** Often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth," the Amazon Rainforest produces 20% of the world's oxygen supply.
5. **The Great Barrier Reef:** Visible from space, the Great Barrier Reef is the largest living structure on Earth, stretching over 2,300 kilometers and comprising thousands of individual reefs and islands.
6. **Migration Marvels:** Many species, from birds to butterflies and whales, undertake incredible migratory journeys, traveling thousands of miles annually.
7. **Oldest Living Organism:** Methuselah, a bristlecone pine tree in California, is over 4,800 years old, making it one of the oldest known living organisms on Earth.
8. **Rainbows:** Rainbows are formed by the reflection, refraction, and dispersion of light in water droplets, creating a stunning spectrum of colors in the sky.
9. **Tidal Energy:** The gravitational pull of the Moon and the Sun creates tides, a powerful and renewable source of energy harnessed by some coastal regions.
10. **Eternal Ice:** Antarctica contains about 60% of the world's fresh water in the form of ice, and some ice cores drilled from Antarctica reveal information about Earth's climate history.
11. **Venomous Plants:** While many animals are venomous, some plants, like the stinging nettle, have specialized cells that inject toxins when touched.
12. **The Dead Sea:** The Dead Sea is the saltiest body of water on Earth, so dense that people can float effortlessly on its surface.

14. **Natural Recyclers:** Decomposers like fungi and bacteria break down organic matter, recycling nutrients back into the ecosystem.
15. **Migration of Monarchs:** Monarch butterflies undertake an incredible migration, traveling up to 4,800 kilometers from Canada and the United States to Mexico for the winter.
